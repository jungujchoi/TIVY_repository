Common Friend and Path

    When two network members are connected to one same member, this member becomes a common friend. Take a look at the node-link diagram on the left side of Fig. 1. Nathan is friends with both Joe and Sue and thus he is a common friend between Joe and Sue. In the matrix visualization on the right side, Sue and Joe's rows/columns are highlighted. It may be difficult to find the common friend from the matrix visualization at first glance, but the matrix visulization tells us who the members' friends are. Joe is friends with Nathan while Sue is friends with Mary, Nathan, Kevin and Colin. And from this, one can find Nathan is the common friend between Joe and Sue.

    Fig. 1. The node-link diagram and the matrix visualization representing a network of seven members. Philip and Nathan have a common friend, Kevin. The friendship between Joe and Nathan (Red) and that between Nathan and Sue (Blue) are shown.

    Next, consider a situation where a network member interacts with another member who is not directly connected to him or her via other network members. For instance, Joe gives his friend Nathan a fun comic book. Having finished readng it, Nathan gives the comic book to Sue, and she passes it on to her friend Colin. Although Joe did not give the comic book to Colin, it ended up in Colin's possession indirectly. Such indirect interaction between two members via other members is considered a "network path."

    Fig. 2. The node-link diagram and the matrix visualization showing a network path between Joe and Colin.

    In the node-link diagram, finding a path between two nodes maybe as easy as looking for possible ways to hop onto neighboring nodes to reach the destination. It is not difficult to see that the connections of Joe-Nathan-Sue-Colin form a path as shown in Fig. 2. The partially highlighted matrix visualization on the right shows one strategy to find such path. In Joe's row, you find that Nathan is Joe's friend. Then you look at Nathan's column and find that Sue is Nathan's friend. And you can see from Sue's row that Colin is Sue's friend, thus finding a network path between Joe and Sue.
