Networks

  1. According to Merriam-Webster, a network is “a usually informally interconnected group or association of persons (such as friends or professional colleagues).” A few good examples of a network include:
    • Class of 2014: individuals who graduated from college in the year 2014
    • A book club: a group of individuals who participate in the book club’s activity
    • American Physical Society: a group of researchers who contribute to physics research through conference and journal publication in North America.
  2. The participants of a certain network naturally share some common interest and goals. It is also often of great interest to analyze how individuals interact with one another within the network. Imagine a book club with five members, Mary, Sue, Nathan, Kevin, and Philip. Within this book club, these individuals form more complicated relationships. For example:
    • Mary and Nathan are friends.
    • Sue and Philip are friends.
    • Mary and Kevin are friends.
    • Kevin and Philip are friends.
    • Nathan and Sue are friends.
